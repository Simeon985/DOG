# LED Emotion Recognition — User Observation Notes 

## Context

Test session to evaluate how well emotions displayed on 8×8 LED matrix eyes are recognized.  
Different emotional patterns were shown, including variations in size and alignment.

---

## Observations

### 1. Heart Pattern

- Interpretation: two staring eyes, uncomfortable feeling
- Perceived emotion: neutral / awkward
- Remark: lack of symmetry was distracting

---

### 2. Sad Pattern

- Interpretation: bear with ears, cute appearance
- Perceived emotion: friendliness / cuteness
- Remark: intention was not immediately clear without explanation

---

### 3. Angry Pattern

- Interpretation: anger (after brief thinking)
- Remark: suggested adding an eyebrow to strengthen emotional clarity

---

### 4. Neutral Pattern

- Interpretation: someone staring clumsily (e.g. fascinated or attracted)
- Perceived emotion: interest / fascination
- Remark: emotion comes across reasonably well; animation would improve it

---

### 5. Sad Pattern (Larger)

- Interpretation: user initially thought it was the same as the previous one
- Recognition difference was unclear

---

### 6. Angry Pattern (Larger)

- Interpretation: emotion became clearer after noticing the increased size
- Remark: user did not immediately perceive the difference

---

### 7. Neutral Pattern (Larger)

- Interpretation: awkward / sad / absent
- Perceived emotion: low engagement, little interaction
- Remark: similar structure to earlier pattern but perceived as looking downward

---

### 8. Heart (Larger)

- Interpretation: affection / being in love
- Remark: this version was clearer and better recognized than pattern 1

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Symmetry is important** for comfort and emotional clarity
- Some emotions require **additional features (e.g. eyebrows)** to be recognized faster
- **Animation could significantly improve perceived intent**
- Users may not immediately notice size changes
- Enlarged heart patterns improve emotional recognition
- Downward gaze patterns reduce perceived interaction
